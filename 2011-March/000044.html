>> Ciao Stefano, We're intrested in using your nice Total Open Station
>> software, and are wondering if it's supported/usable for any of the
>> top selling models on amazon.com, like CST/berger 5-Second Electronic
>> Total Station - Model# 56-CST205
> 
> first of all thanks for your interest in Total Open Station. As you
> might have guessed from the website, the software is still quite
> experimental, in that it has been tested with very few models and
> makers. CST/berger is not (yet!) among those, unfortunately. I wasn't
> able to find detailed specs on their website, but the main thing to
> check is whether it's using some strange proprietary communication
> protocol or a standard serial communication. Once the data are
> downloaded, there may be thousands of different formats in which they
> come, but it's a matter of writing the right software module to extract
> it.
> 
>> If not, what models/software would you recommend we use? We definitely
>> want an open linux platform rather than Windows.
> 
> I should say that I'm no expert in total stations ‒ TOPS was written
> primarily to deal with a single device and afterwards other people asked
> us to support more models. It would be good if you could check with
> someone who already has one from CST/berger, at least with regard to the
> TS-PC connection (Total Open Station can be used as a "dumb" serial
> receiving terminal when in "Custom" mode).
> 
> Generally speaking, old devices are much simpler in their machinery,
> while new ones tend to have "cool" features (e.g. Bluetooth data
> transmission) that are more difficult to support in a general purpose,
> low-profile application like TOPS.
> 
> After all, I think that you could be able to make a good contribution to
> Total Open Station by helping with extending support to this new model.
> 
> Please let me know if you need more information.
