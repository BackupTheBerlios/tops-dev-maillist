To make it short: I used the pep8.py tool to check and repair our source
tree for common PEP-8 faults.

http://www.python.org/dev/peps/pep-0008/ is Guido's Style Guide for
Python Code, the benefits of using it as a standard style are obvious,
and make for better readability and consistency of our code.

You can get pep8.py at http://github.com/jcrocholl/pep8 or from Debian
sid or from Ubuntu Lucid at http://packages.ubuntu.com/lucid/pep8 and
probably in many other places.

I'm trying to see if it can be inserted into a Mercurial pre-commit hook
to check before all commits. Until I find something however I recommend
to run it manually on files you've changed before committing.
